1. Abstract

Biti (BITI) is an ERC-20 token deployed on the Polygon blockchain, inspired by Bitcoin's core principles of transparency, decentralization, and scarcity. With an initial supply of 99 billion units, a deflationary burn mechanism, founder vesting, and built-in security protections, BITI is designed to be a robust, auditable, and accessible digital asset for the new generation of DeFi participants.

Biti is not a mere copy — it is a modern reinterpretation of Bitcoin's principles built on Polygon's fast, low-cost infrastructure.

2. Introduction

Since Bitcoin's launch in 2009, the crypto asset market has grown exponentially. However, the technical barrier to creating, distributing, and using tokens remains high for most of the population. Biti was created with the mission of simplifying access to the DeFi (Decentralized Finance) ecosystem, offering an audited, transparent token with clear rules from day one.

Built on the ERC-20 standard and OpenZeppelin v5 libraries — the most audited in the ecosystem — BITI is verifiable by anyone on Polygonscan and can be traded directly on Uniswap without intermediaries.

3. The Problem

The crypto ecosystem faces three critical challenges that prevent mass adoption:

  • Lack of transparency: Many projects neither publish nor verify their smart contract source code.
  • Security risks: Unaudited contracts are vulnerable to attacks, resulting in billions of dollars lost every year.
  • Concentration of power: Projects where the team can arbitrarily manipulate the supply or pause transactions without prior communication.

4. The Solution

Biti addresses these challenges with four pillars:

  1. Open Source and Verified: Contract published and verified on Polygonscan. Anyone can read and audit the code.
  2. Security by Design: Based on OpenZeppelin v5 with documented internal audit (AUDIT_REPORT.md). Vulnerability BITI-01 was identified and fixed before deployment.
  3. Clear Supply Rules: Absolute cap of 110 billion. Burn mechanism available to all holders. 6-month vesting for founders.
  4. Unrestricted Access: Tradeable directly on Uniswap. One-click MetaMask integration. No KYC, no intermediaries.

Fee Mechanism

The contract allows setting a transaction fee between 0% and 5%. When active, a fraction of each transfer is sent to the owner's address. This feature can be used to fund development, marketing, or token buyback and burn.

Deflationary Burn

Any holder can call the burn() function to permanently destroy their own tokens, reducing the total supply. This creates a voluntary deflationary mechanism that may appreciate remaining tokens over time.

6. Technology

Blockchain

BITI is deployed on the Polygon blockchain — a high-performance Layer 2 network fully compatible with Ethereum, offering extremely low transaction fees and fast confirmation times.

ERC-20 Standard

ERC-20 is the de facto standard for fungible tokens on Ethereum. It guarantees compatibility with all wallets (MetaMask, Ledger, etc.) and DEXs (Uniswap, SushiSwap, etc.).

OpenZeppelin v5

OpenZeppelin libraries are audited by over 300 security researchers and used by projects managing over $1 trillion in assets. Biti inherits protections from:

  • ERC20Pausable — global emergency pause
  • ERC20Burnable — token burn
  • ERC20Capped — hard supply cap
  • Ownable — access control
  • ReentrancyGuard — reentrancy attack protection

Development Stack

  • Solidity 0.8.20 with optimizer enabled
  • Hardhat 2.22.x with ESM
  • Automated tests: 14/14 passing
  • Polygonscan verification with hardhat-verify

7. Security

The contract underwent an internal audit prior to deployment. Vulnerability BITI-01 was identified and resolved. The full report is available in AUDIT_REPORT.md in the repository.

BITI-01 (Fixed)

The _update() function was calling ERC20._update directly instead of super._update, which allowed the transaction fee to be charged even when the contract was paused. Fixed before mainnet deployment.

BITI-02, 03, 04 (Documented Design Risks)

Centralization risks documented: the owner can pause, change the fee, and mint up to the hard cap. These powers are intentional and publicly documented for full transparency.

Future Security Plans

The team plans to engage a certified auditor such as Certik, Hacken, or Trail of Bits in Phase 4 of the roadmap.
